What is
this
Document 
About?		 This document supports organization's effort to meet the ongoing challenge of providing software projects and services to clients or internal use that meet their continually changing needs.

The Quality Method of Operation (QMO) is a predictable, repeatable process designed to produce continually improving quality software projects, thus ensuring client satisfaction. The seven QMO phases do not imply a sequential order. Steps and activities may overlap and may be performed concurrently. For example, a section that lists a draft document as its Entrance Criteria implies that there is an overlap with the section requiring the final version of the same document.

The QMO: 

  • emphasizes client involvement throughout 
  • stresses building in quality up front 
  • specifies accountability 
  • defines entrance and exit criteria for each life cycle phase 
  • employs "teaming" as a key aspect of project management 
  • provides templates to be used for key outputs 
  • uses baselining and change control to manage change.
Who Is 
The QMO 
For?		 The QMO is for anyone involved in initiating, defining, designing, building, integrating, testing, deploying, maintaining, supporting or selecting products for company's Network, Service Delivery and User Access Centers of Excellence (COE) software projects.

 
 
Organization 
Mgmt 
Commitment 		 The QMO have to be endorsed by the company and is supported by the Quality Management Team (QMT).

 
 
QMO 
Conformance 
Policy		 Company is committed to improving the quality, productivity, and predictability of projects. To support this goal, all software projects within the three COEs are required to comply with the QMO, which outlines a software development methodology. 

Audits and management reviews will verify compliance with the QMO.

Training, tools, and techniques will be provided, as needed, to ensure compliance.

Approval for QMO exceptions is obtained from the QMT and copies of approved exceptions are sent to the originator. Exceptions will be kept to a minimum and only approved where absolutely necessary.

As the QMO is updated, a "Compliance date required" notation is issued to call attention to high impact QMO changes. Projects may elect to comply with these QMO changes prior to the compliance date. However, once the compliance date is reached, all of the updated QMO activities will be required. 

The implementation methodology is, "Don't look back". History is not re-written. Pick up where you are on the project. 
QMO 
Implementations 		 As previously stated, all software development done by the three COEs is expected to follow the process outlined in the QMO. There are several valid implementations of the QMO: iterative, incremental, spiral, prototype, waterfall, relaxed waterfall, etc. 

Please see the Job Aid, for Implementation of the QMO for Small Projects, Rapid Development Projects, Prototyping and Unplanned Maintenance Releases for implementation tips in these situations. 

 
 
Release 
Schedule		 Client needs and Compan's business drivers, coupled with environment realities and resources, determine release schedules through negotiation and sign off by all affected parties.

 
 
Controlled Documents
and Quality 
Records 		 Controlled documents are those documents that may result from a key project deliverable. Examples include Requirements, Detailed Designs, etc. They are baselined and follow a numbering scheme. After baselining, all changes use the change control process. 

The controlled documents required in the QMO must be presented using the templates provided. 

Some of the controlled documents are reviewed by our clients. When this is the case, the documents must be sent to the clients with enough lead time to allow them to do a thorough review.

All projects are expected to maintain the quality records and controlled documents required by the QMO and in accordance with Company's record retention policies. These documents/artifacts are the evidence that demonstrate conformance to the QMO. Local Procedures are controlled documents.

 
 
Templates The templates listed in the QMO contain the minimally required data content and format to be used. Any project wishing to utilize a template outside of the QMO is required to gain approval for such use from their Process Improvement Team (PIT). Once approved, a customized template may be used going forward and should be documented in the project's local procedures. Customized templates must always comply with the minimum data content found in the QMO template. 

When using a template, it is acceptable to point to other documents or electronic locations to complete a section. For example, staffing information needed on a Master Test Plan may point a reader to the project's Project Plan. 

 
 
Tools Where noted throughout the QMO, these items are suggestions and not requirements..However, a project's local procedures may note a specific requirement. 

 
 
Techniques Where noted throughout the QMO, these items are suggestions and not requirements. However, a project's local procedures may note a specific requirement. 

 
Project 
Specific 
Documentation 		 Projects are responsible for documenting the following at the Project level (local procedures):
  • Organization Chart 
  • Implementation of Library (e.g., index, access, filing, storage, maintenance and disposition of quality records, controlled documents and project specific procedures) 
  • Project Training needs 
  • Root Cause Analysis practices for Change Requests (CRs) 
  • Project Procedure Documents (e.g., Coding Standards, Service Level Agreements, Estimation) 
  • Control over Contractors and Subcontractors (if applicable)* 
  • Approved, Customized Templates* 
  • Traceability procedures from Requirements to Design to Test and from Test back to Requirements.* 
  • Functional processes or procedures beyond the QMO*. 
  • Quality Improvement Plan (QUIP) 
  • Project processes for estimating size, effort, and cost.* 


    • * May not be applicable to projects that are capped (defined as waiting to be retired) or in maintenance mode (defined as a project that is not developing new code, only fixing high severity problems). 

 
 
Project 
Management 		 All projects should follow sound project management processes. These should be documented as part of a project's local procedures.

QMO Change Request  Updates to the QMO are managed and coordinated by the PIT. Changes should be forwarded to your PIT representatives. If submitting a QMO change request, include: 
  • requestor's name and address (may be electronic address) 
  • the date the request is made 
  • a description of the change 
  • a brief explanation of why the change is needed 
  • an indication of the priority or severity of the request 
Once received, the QMO change request will be tracked and evaluated. The QMT will vote on the QMO change request. The vote will be final. Change requests will either be incorporated in future QMO releases, or classified as no change required.
