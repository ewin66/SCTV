Jeopardy Process

Introduction
 
Purpose  The Jeopardy Process is a project tracking vehicle to provide adequate visibility and alert management early so effective corrective actions can be taken when a project deviates significantly from project plans thereby placing one or more of the following at risk: 
  • Schedules 
  • Cost/Estimate 
  • Deliverables (from start of work or Project Plan) 
  • Quality 
  • Client Satisfaction 


Software development activities are monitored by project management. Progress is determined by comparing project actuals against project plans. When it is determined that a project is going beyond manageable risk or is outside acceptable variance levels, a jeopardy shall be written to alert management and possibly the client. In addition, an effective corrective action plan is developed to bring the risk area back under control.

Jeopardy Conditions
Jeopardy Conditions Overview  This section details the conditions under which a jeopardy must be initiated. Mandatory jeopardies shall be classified in two status categories and shall be initiated based on project variance from estimated targets in four major areas. A jeopardy may also be initiated at the discretion of project management for any other factor or condition that may put the project beyond manageable risk. These conditions include but are not limited to a hostile client relationship, contractual (Project Plan deliverables), schedule, estimate, or other issues open longer than three months. 
Red Status  A red jeopardy status is a serious condition in which one or more project variance conditions outlined below are at risk and recovery from the condition is not likely. Both project and Information Technologies management (Senior Directors) are alerted/informed of the condition through submission of a Jeopardy Notice. A corrective action plan shall be developed and monitored until the condition no longer exists or is within acceptable limits. The table below defines the project variance conditions for red status jeopardies: 
Project Variance Project Variance Description For RED Status Project Variance Cleared or reduced to Yellow Status Comments
Schedule Projected failure to meet key project deliverables or milestones Key project deliverables or milestones have been met and the project is back on schedule Jeopardies should not necessarily be written for slipped dates of individual tasks. Slipped dates for individual tasks may warrant a yellow status.
Cost Estimate to complete is greater than the baselined estimate Estimate to complete is within an acceptable variance of the baselined estimate range May require the project to re-estimate project costs, schedule, etc. for the remainder of the project schedule
Quality Project quality, determined by defects found, is not in the range expected The cause of the high or low defect rate is determined and shown not to put the project at risk Projects shall estimate defects for all QMO defined development life cycle phases (define, design, build, and test) and compare the estimates to the actual defects to determine the quality variance
Technical Projected failure to meet key technical performance requirements (e.g., response time, capacity, etc.) Project demonstrates the ability to meet the key technical performance milestones Technical performance requirements should be defined and documented in a project's baselined requirements and design documents. System interfaces should also be considered at this point.
Other Dependent upon the jeopardy. Dependent upon the jeopardy. Information should be detailed in the Project Plan or in a projects' local procedures.
Yellow Status  A yellow jeopardy status is a potentially serious condition in which one or more project variance conditions below are at risk. Yellow status jeopardies are kept within the project team (Project Directors) and tracked through normal project management at project status meetings. A corrective action plan maintained at the project level is developed and monitored until the condition no longer exists or is within acceptable limits. The table below defines the project variance conditions for yellow status jeopardies: 
Project Variance Project Variance Description For YELLOW Status Project Variance Cleared  Comments
Schedule Projected failure to meet minor project milestones or schedule slippage conditions are occurring to put project milestones at risk Minor project milestones have been met and the project is back on schedule Jeopardies should not necessarily be written for slipped dates of individual tasks. Slipped dates on an individual task may not warrant a jeopardy.
Cost Estimate to complete is greater than the baselined estimate  Estimate to complete is back within tolerable limits  May require the project to re-estimate project cost, schedule, etc. for the remainder of the project schedule 
Quality Project quality, determined by defects found, is greater or lower than expected  The cause of the high or low defect rate is determined and shown not to put the project at risk  Projects shall estimate defects for all QMO defined development life cycle phases (define, design, build, and test) and compare the estimates to the actual defects to determine the quality variance 
Technical Project fails to meet minor technical performance requirements (e.g., response time, capacity, etc.)  Project demonstrates the ability to meet the minor technical performance milestones  Technical performance requirements should be defined and documented in a project's baselined requirements and design documents. System interfaces should also be considered at this point. 
Other Dependent upon the jeopardy. Dependent upon the jeopardy. Information should be detailed in the Project Plan or in a projects' local procedures.

Roles and Responsibilities
Originator Anyone (originator) can initiate the jeopardy process through the Project Manager. The Project Manager must initiate a jeopardy when any of the conditions exist that are noted in the project plan. The originator makes an assessment regarding the jeopardy type and status. This determines the degree of seriousness and initial client impact of the jeopardy. The originator documents and informs the Project Manager that a jeopardy condition exists. 
Project Manager The project manager is accountable for the jeopardy process for the project. Projects may assign jeopardy responsibilities to another project team member (e.g., Release Manager). This situation must then be documented in the Project Plan. 

The Project Manager has the following jeopardy responsibilities: 

  1. Ensures a jeopardy is initiated when any of the conditions warrant. 
  2. Has the principal responsibility for the assessment of the impact the jeopardy has on the client(s), if any. 
  3. Identifies the impacted client(s). 
  4. Identifies an owner for the action plan and oversees the development of the action plan. 
  5. Identifies the appropriate internal distribution of the jeopardy. 
  6. Develops the client notification strategy for the jeopardy. The Project Manager should confer with the upper management and other appropriate persons to determine when and how the client should be notified. The client notification strategy shall be strictly adhered to. Only those people identified as part of this strategy should discuss the jeopardy with the client. 
  7. Ensures that the action plan is tracked, executed as described, and updated as necessary. 
Note: the project manager is responsible for socializing all documented, potential jeopardy situations with project team members prior to accepting or rejecting any such jeopardy. 
Action Plan Owner The action plan owner is identified by the Project Manager and has the following jeopardy responsibilities: 
  1. Develops the action plan, usually with the assistance of the Project Manager, to resolve the jeopardy condition. 
  2. Confirms that the jeopardy notice has been received and reviewed by the people identified on the internal jeopardy distribution list. 
  3. Reviews comments from the people identified on the internal jeopardy distribution list. 
  4. Ensures that the action plan is on schedule and is reviewed/revised during project status meetings. 
  5. Keeps the Project Manager informed regarding the status of the action plan. 
  6. Once the updated schedule is confirmed, closes the jeopardy.

Jeopardy Process Steps

This section details the steps to be followed by project teams to initiate a red status jeopardy. At the discretion of the project manager, a yellow status jeopardy may also follow this structure and formality.
Jeopardy Process Steps
  1. Identify/acknowledge that a jeopardy condition exists. 
  2. The jeopardy originator assesses the status of the jeopardy (red/yellow). 
  3. The jeopardy originator authors the jeopardy. 
  4. The jeopardy originator confers with the Project Manager to determine the action plan owner. 
  5. The action plan to resolve the jeopardy condition is developed. 
  6. The Project Manager, based on discussions with the originator, Senior Directors, and other appropriate persons, determines if the jeopardy has critical impact on the client(s). 
  7. If the jeopardy has critical impact on the client(s), a Jeopardy Impact Statement is developed. The impact statement addresses the business impact on the client(s) and the impact on our client relationship (e.g., loss of credibility). 
  8. The jeopardy is distributed according to the internal distribution list. Minimally, the internal distribution list shall include: 
    • The impacted project(s) and other appropriate managers 
    • If the jeopardy has critical client impact, the Corporate Vice President - Quality 
  9. The action plan owner confirms that the jeopardy notice has been received by the people identified on the internal distribution list and reviews all comments regarding the jeopardy. 
  10. The action plan owner executes the plan according to the schedule and files updates to the jeopardy. 
  11. When the action plan is complete, the jeopardy is closed by the action plan owner. The owner ensures that the actual closed date is recorded in the jeopardy. If the jeopardy was discussed with the client, the Project Manager ensures that the client is informed that the jeopardy has been closed. 
  12. The action plan owner, based on discussions with the originator and the Project Manager, determines the root cause of the jeopardy and uses this analysis as input to the Lessons Learned Session.

Key Considerations
The following are key considerations involved with initiating a jeopardy 
Key Considerations
  1. All employees are empowered to initiate a jeopardy, even if the jeopardy is not against a key milestone or commitment. 
  2. A jeopardy should be initiated as early as possible to allow as much advance notice to those impacted and to enhance the availability of options and resources to resolve the jeopardy condition. A project should not wait until a commitment date is near or missed to initiate a jeopardy. 
  3. Thought should be given to determine what warrants a jeopardy outside of the mandatory conditions. For example, if a product is several days late entering a particular activity, but the critical path is not seriously impacted and quality is not at risk, a jeopardy need not be written. If resources can be moved to resolve a problem, a jeopardy need not be written. These situations should be handled through normal project management. Use common sense when determining whether a jeopardy should be initiated. 
  4. The jeopardy notice should be completely filled out and clearly describe the jeopardy condition. 
  5. Action plans should be developed that are clearly and easily understood and executable with measurable and obtainable goals. If the action plan calls for a change control to the baselined schedule, the jeopardy can be closed with the approval of the new schedule and a reasonable number of milestones completed. The new schedule should be used at all future project team meetings. 
  6. The Client Notification Strategy shall be strictly adhered to. Only those people identified as part of the strategy should discuss the jeopardy with the client(s).

Root Cause Analysis (RCA)
RCA Overview The Project Manager or designated Subject Matter Experts (SME) shall perform a root cause analysis for all mandatory jeopardies as part of the normal jeopardy process. The root cause analysis determines the root cause of the jeopardy and uses this information as input to the Formal Lessons Learned Session.
