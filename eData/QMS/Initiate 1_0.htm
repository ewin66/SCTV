Estimation  Use the estimation spreadsheet as a basis for beginning the estimation process. Complete the estimate, including risks and assumptions. Retain the estimate risks, and asumptions in the Project Plan or other medium. 

Track, review, and update estimates, risks, and assumptions at Project Status Meetings. 

The spreadsheet labeled QMOEST provides the ability to estimate on a feature level based on historical development effort heuristics done for a two year period on a yearly basis. Effort hours may be estimated for the development function where estimates for the other software functions may then be expressed as a percentage of development effort. 

This QMO Estimation Spreadsheet is to be used to develop bottom-up estimates of effort and cost.

Project Status Meetings  Project team members continually perform traditional project management functions to ensure the quality of the process and the project. All monitoring and controlling functions are the responsibility of the Project Manager. 
Steps
  1. Schedule project status meetings on a regular basis (e.g., bi-weekly, monthly) or as a particular need arises. Frequency may be determined by the complexity, size or risk factors of the project. 
  2. Develop a standard meeting agenda to ensure that the following topics are regularly covered: 
    • planning and organizing resources 
    • risk assessment and contingency planning 
    • monitoring and reviewing status (including schedule updates, documentation status and software baseline status) 
    • resolving issues 
    • performing cost and schedule review 
    • managing and informing key stakeholders and affected parties of Statement of Work (SOW) amendments or other project changes 
    • evaluating estimates and re-estimating if appropriate 
    • assigning and closing action items 
    • opening and tracking Jeopardies 
    • performing financial management 
    • managing client and third party relations 
    • reviewing progress of the project's Quality Improvement Plan (found in Appendix) 
  3. Document important discussions in meeting minutes and maintain them in the product/project library along with Action Item Register. 
  4. Update the Project Plan as needed.
