Internal QMO Audit Procedures
1.0 Auditing Information 
QMO Audits are a systematic and independent examination of an organization's procedures to determine whether activities and related results are used effectively, are suitable to achieve the organization's objectives and to determine if established procedures are in compliance with the current QMO. Audits tell the Project and the Quality Management Team (QMT) whether the QMO is achieving the planned objectives in an effective manner and which areas need improvement.
1.1 Objectives
The QMO Audit Procedure applies to all the Network, Service Delivery, and User Access COEs (herein referred to as "the three COEs" involved in any aspect of the development, delivery, and ongoing support of software products. Initially, the purpose of the audits will be to determine if software organizations have begun to follow the QMO. Once organizations are following QMO, the purpose of the audit program will shift to review the effectiveness of the QMO implementation. Results from audits will be used to provide feedback for QMO enhancements.
1.2 Scope
The QMO Audit Procedure will focus on the implementation and operation of the processes documented in the current QMO. The audits will also review local procedures. Audits will focus on the software development and/or maintenance or enhancement organization. That is, all software-related work within an organization will be subject to the audit. Work within organizations will be reviewed from a management perspective. (For example, how is the work within an organization managed?) Audits will review the work on a going-forward basis, reviewing work from the specific phase that the work or project is in when the QMO is first introduced. While projects are not required to go back and create a work product/artifacts for a previous phase, they are required to understand what the QMO requirements are for previous phases and be able to explain what they would do the next time they are in that phase.

All projects begun after the introduction of the QMO are expected to be fully compliant with the requirements of the QMO.

All Director organizations within the three COEs that are doing software development and/or maintenance are subject to audit. All major work activities within the organizations will be audited. If there are multiple work activities within an organization that are managed differently, multiple audits may be necessary.
1.3 Frequency Guidelines
The frequency of QMO Audits conducted on software organizations is based on several factors:

  • Results of previous QMO audits
  • Current criticality of project or function
  • Directed by COE Management, QMT, or other Senior Directors
  • Results of CMM based Assessments
To satisfy the above guidelines, an annual audit schedule will be developed and maintained by the Managers of the QMO Audit Groups.

All organizations are responsible for compliance with the QMO. Accordingly, all organizations are subject to audits. That is, new development and enhancement work to existing systems should all follow the guidelines set forth in the QMO. A minimum of one week's notice will be given to organizations prior to the audit.
1.4 Responsibility
The COE Governance Team has the overall management responsibility for the QMO Audit Group for all organizations involved in development and maintenance. The COE Governance Team approves all changes to this document using the change control procedures described in the QMO.

The Managers of the QMO Audit Groups have the overall responsibility for the development and maintenance of the audit schedule.

The Lead Auditor is responsible for producing and distributing the final audit report and updating the status of non-conformance items.

The three COEs' Senior Directors are responsible for assuring that their work groups are in compliance with the QMO. They are also responsible for enabling the auditors in the audit process.
1.5 Audit Quality Assurance
It is the responsibility of the Managers of the QMO Audit Groups to continually monitor the effectiveness of the audit program, recommend plans needed to maintain the competency of all auditors, and take any appropriate actions to ensure that the three COEs continue to benefit from the high quality and value of the audit program.
1.6 Configuration Management of Audit Documents
Audit reports will be retained according to the document control procedures described in the QMO.
2.0 Audit Team Characteristics
QMO Audits are conducted by an audit team under the leadership of a Lead Auditor. Lead Auditors and Auditors are independent of the projects being audited. An audit team consists of at least one Lead Auditor and may include other auditors, subject matter experts (i.e. middle manager or other participants who have received auditing training), auditor trainees, and/or observers. Team composition is at the discretion of the Managers of the QMO Audit Groups and the Lead Auditor. All interviews and discussions during the audits will be completely confidential. Reports will identify issues and not individuals.

Directors, Senior Directors, and QMT members will be encouraged to participate on audits in areas other than their own. Observers are allowed on audit teams in organizations other than their own. However, managers cannot serve in observer roles.
2.1 Lead Auditor
A Lead Auditor is an individual who has the necessary qualifications and is authorized by the three COEs to lead QMO Audits.

Telcordia Technologies Qualified Lead Auditors will lead the QMO audits for 1999.
2.1.1 Responsibilities
The Lead Auditor is responsible for:

  • Finalizing the audit schedule with the audited organization
  • Developing an audit plan (if different from a standard QMO audit)
  • Ensuring adherence to the audit plan
  • Managing the activities of the audit team
  • Conducting the opening meeting (if scheduled)
  • Presenting the results of the audit at the closing meeting
  • Documenting and distributing the results of the audit in an audit report
  • Scheduling the follow-up audit (if necessary)
  • Following-up on activities associated with any action plan developed as a result of the audit
2.1.2 Qualifications
A Lead Auditor must meet the following qualifications:
  • Must have successfully completed a Lead Auditor training course
  • Have two or more years of software development experience in one or more of the following disciplines: project management, requirements specification, architecture and design, implementation, testing, configuration management, product support, or software testing
  • Must have participated in at least 5 QMO audits under the guidance of a qualified Lead Auditor, or be certified as a Lead Auditor by an external audit program and have participated on at least one QMO audit
  • Possess a thorough knowledge of the QMO
2.2 Audit Team
The audit team consists of individuals with software development expertise and/or individuals that can provide value to the audit program. The audit team should have a minimum of 2 auditors (one Lead Auditor). The normal audit team size is two. One observer may also participate following normal rules for an observer (i.e. they observe and do not participate). The purposes for an observer may be for an individual to learn more about the auditing process and what is required to be compliant with QMO. Observers must be approved by the Managers of the QMO Audit Groups with concurrence of the Lead Auditor in advance of the audit.
2.2.1 Responsibilities
The audit team is responsible for:
  • Assisting and providing support to the Lead Auditor
  • Participating in all required interview sessions as directed by the Lead Auditor
  • Reviewing project documents as directed by the Lead Auditor
  • Assisting the Lead Auditor in the writing of the audit report
  • Coordinating responses/corrective actions associated with audit findings
  • Performing additional activities normally performed by the Lead Auditor to prepare the team auditors to become Lead Auditors
2.2.2 Qualifications
An audit team member must meet the following qualifications:
  • Should have successfully completed a Lead Auditor training course (if possible).
  • Have two or more years of software development experience in one or more of the following disciplines: project management, requirements specification, architecture and design, implementation, testing, configuration management or product support
  • Possess knowledge of QMO
2.3 Audited Organization
The Audited Organization is the organization and individuals to be audited. The Audited Organization appoints a representative who is responsible for:
  • Working with the Lead Auditor to finalize the audit schedule
  • Informing all audit participants of the audit schedule
  • Arranging all audit logistics related to project personnel and materials (Including meeting rooms, computer access to project work products, participation of project personnel, etc.) not expected to supply Audit team with tools for report writing, etc.
  • Providing the Lead Auditor with required quality system documentation prior to the audit
3.0 The Audit Process
This section describes audit planning and preparation. To be effective, an audit must have careful planning and preparation. This phase of the Quality System Audit is a substantial task and is carried out predominantly by the Lead Auditor.
3.1 Audit Types
3.1.1 QMO Audits
Projects will be audited across the three COEs. This will be the predominant type of audit. QMO audits will be performed to verify that work within the organization is managed in such a way as to conform to the requirements of QMO.
3.1.2 Director Audits
Director audits will be performed on a sampling basis. These audits will be initially conducted during the month that the managers have received QMO training. The purpose of Director audits is to determine if they understand what they are required to do as a result of the QMO management training. If not, the audit team should help them understand what is required and what they should be doing.

Director audits will review:

  • management commitment to QMO compliance
  • action plans for implementing QMO
  • progress monitoring and corrections to plans for implementing QMO
  • resources assigned to implement QMO
  • practitioner perceptions of management support in implementing and following QMO.
Review of management support and commitment will also be part of QMO audits.
3.2 Audit Schedule
The Managers of the QMO Audit Groups schedule timeframes for QMO audits based on the guidelines specified in Section 1.3. The audit schedule will include all Director organizations within the three COEs. The Managers of the QMO Audit Groups establish audit teams consisting of a Lead Auditor and other qualified auditors as determined by the scope of the audit.

The Managers of the QMO Audit Groups will also resolve any disputes/conflicts regarding the audit schedule, the audit team participants, and the audited organizations.
3.3 Audit Preparation
The Lead Auditor finalizes an interview schedule with the audited organization. The interview schedule minimally includes the initial audit interview and closing conference. The Lead Auditor, in conjunction with the audit team, prepares a checklist as a guide for the audit. The checklist is consistent with the Entrance Criteria, Exit Criteria and Quality Records identified in the QMO. The Lead Auditor is responsible for defining the scope and objectives of the audit with the audited organizations' management.

The Lead Auditor communicates with the audited team representative, confirming the schedule, scope, and objectives, and describing the activities planned, before the audit. On agreement, the audited team representative distributes a schedule to all of the participants at least two days before the audit.
3.4 Opening Meeting
An optional opening meeting, which is conducted by the Lead Auditor, is primarily for the benefit of the audited organization. It provides the forum to:

  • Introduce all parties associated with the audit
  • Explain the audit scope and objectives
  • Explain how the audit will be conducted
  • Explain the expected output of the audit (e.g., positive observations, opportunities for improvement, non-conformance items)
  • Confirm audit schedule
  • Answer questions/concerns from the audited organization
The meeting typically lasts less than a half-hour depending on questions. It establishes a rapport with the audited organization and sets a positive tone for the remaining audit activities.
3.5 Conduct the Audit
The audit team will review artifacts, or work products, prior to the interview portion of audits.

The audit team interviews selected audit participants. There are three areas to record information and findings:

  • Positive Observations - processes and procedures that work well.
  • Opportunities for improvement - areas of concern not directly traceable to the QMO, but which potentially could improve product quality or reduce the overall cost of quality.
  • Non-conformance items to the QMO - non-conformance items are recorded as either major or minor findings.
    • Major findings - procedures are consistently not followed, or procedures are non-existent, or a failure to meet requirements. Major findings require a formal action plan.
    • Minor findings - isolated instances where procedures are not followed (a minor failure to comply with the defined process could have a major impact on Quality). Minor findings do not require a formal action plan to be developed.
The audit team strives to put the audited organization at ease by establishing a positive rapport and responding to any questions or concerns they may have before starting the interview. Through the use of the audit checklist or other mechanism, the auditor asks for objective evidence (e.g., documentation, Quality Records) to ensure that the organization is successfully implementing and following the QMO. All findings, both positive and negative, are recorded for discussion with the audit team and as input to the interim feedback sessions, the closing meeting, and the final audit report.
3.6 Interim Feedback Sessions (during the audit)
The audit team may schedule optional informal interim feedback sessions with the audited team's management representative plus other audit participants to discuss the progress of the audit. These meetings are normally scheduled at the end of each day of interviews or the beginning of the next day. At these meetings, all observations of effective processes and procedures, opportunities for improvement, and any non-conformances are reviewed to obtain agreement with the audited organization and to clarify any issues that may arise from the observations. The combined observations from all interim feedback sessions are the main input to the closing meeting and the final audit report.
3.7 Closing Meeting
The audit team meets before the closing meeting, typically for an hour or so, to prepare and review the information to be presented at the closing meeting. The closing meeting provides a summary of the audit for the audited organization and provides closure to the process. The closing meeting:
  • Recognizes the audit participants
  • Summarizes the processes that the audited organization is performing well
  • Summarizes the areas and opportunities for improvement
  • Summarizes the QMO non-conformance items
  • Reviews the corrective action/follow-up process (if necessary)
  • Responds to any final questions from the audited organization
  • Schedules a follow-up date to resolve non-conformance items (if necessary)
The auditors will know QMO requirements and will provide guidance during the audit. Audited teams are allowed, and encouraged, to correct situations during the audit if possible and if it is meaningful to do so.
3.8 Audit Report
The final audit report is prepared by the Lead Auditor with the assistance of the audit team. The audit report provides a balanced and informative picture of the total state of the organization's procedures and includes the positive observations, opportunities for improvement, and the QMO non-conformance items resulting from the audit investigation. The report should be concise and to the point and include the following information:
  • An overview that includes an introduction, a statement of the purpose and scope of the audit, and a statement of the audit plan used as a basis for the audit
  • A general comments section that summarizes the positive observations, recommended opportunities for improvement, and the quality system non-conformances
The final audit report is completed and distributed to the audited organization within ten working days from the date of the closing meeting. The final audit report is also used by the tactical arm of the QMT to develop audit metrics for input into the QMT Formal Management Review process. The audit report will be confidential to the organization that was audited. The results of reports will be summarized and shared with the QMT.
3.9 Corrective Action Follow-up
The audited organization is responsible for the development of a corrective action plan based on any major non-conformance items documented in the final audit report. For each non-conformance item, the audited organization analyzes the problem, determines the root cause, and develops a corrective action plan to resolve the problem. The Lead Auditor who performed the audit or the Managers of the QMO Audit Groups should approve the action plan. All corrective action plans must be implemented within one month of the date the corrective action plans are approved. If corrective action plans are not implemented and closed within two months, the Lead Auditor escalates the open action plan issues following the escalation procedure described in Section 3.9.1 - Escalate Findings.

If the action plans are not acceptable, the Lead Auditor explains why the proposed action plan is not satisfactory and works with the audited organization to modify and improve the action plans until they are acceptable.

If the action plans are acceptable, the Lead Auditor updates the non-conformance database with the approved action plans. The Lead Auditor schedules a follow-up visit with the audited organization to ensure that the action plans have been implemented and to determine the effectiveness of the corrective action. During the follow up visit, the Lead Auditor restricts the investigation to obtaining status and performance (Are they doing it the way they said they would?) information of the action plans. If action plans remain open after two months, the issue(s) are escalated following the escalation procedure described in Section 3.9.1 - Escalate Findings.
3.9.1 Escalate Findings
During the follow up of corrective action, there may be situations that require escalation. These situations include, but are not limited to the following:

  • The audited organization does not submit an immediate/preventive action plan within 10 workings days from the issuance of the final audit report to the Lead Auditor
  • The audited organization does not agree with the decision of the Lead Auditor to reject the immediate/preventive action plan and does not resubmit a corrective action plan
  • A second request for a plan after an inadequate plan was submitted does not result in an acceptable plan
  • A follow up visit(s) is conducted and found that corrective action was not taken within two months or the corrective action was not effective
The Lead Auditor informs the Managers of the QMO Audit Groups of any situation that requires escalation. If the Managers of the QMO Audit Groups approve the need to escalate findings, then the following actions are taken:
  • The audited organization's Director is informed in advance of escalating findings and the audited organization is notified of escalation.
  • The COE Governance Team is informed of the open action items and decides the next steps. Normally, all non-conformance items will be addressed by the COE Governance Team on a periodic basis. Non-conformance items may also be escalated on an event-driven basis.
3.9.2 Closing the Audit
Non-conformance reports are considered closed when the Lead Auditor verifies completed corrective action, completes the "Corrective Action Verified By" section of the non-conformance report and returns it to the audited organization. Once all non-conformance reports are closed, follow up with the audited organization for the audit is ended.

The decisions of the COE Governance Team and COE PIT Managers of the QMO Audit Groups are final.

The Lead Auditor ensures that all updates to the non-conformance reports are closed and informs the COE PIT Managers and Managers of the QMO Audit Groups.
3.10 Audit Review
On a regular basis, the Managers of the QMO Audit Groups and audit team members will review the results of the audits conducted and the outstanding non-conformance items from previous audits. The output of the review will be audit metrics for the QMT Formal COE/PIT Covernance Team Review process. During the audit review, non-conformance items from individual organization audits will be examined for trends that would indicate systemic problems across an organization. The findings from the review, as well as applicable historical findings, will be used to determine if a finding is systemic in nature. It is at this time that findings from individual organization audits may be classified as major and brought to the attention of the COE PIT/Governance Team as part of the normal reporting and escalation process.
