Introduction This Quality Manual provides an overview of the quality policy, management responsibility, and approach to quality. The audience and users of this manual are all members of the Network, Service Delivery and User Access Centers of Excellence. 

This Quality System: 

  • Assures existing and potential clients that quality is our number one priority 
  • Provides descriptions of our quality vision 
  • Enables organizations to develop projects and services based on a solid quality foundation
The Vice President (VP) of Quality role, which is currently being filled by representatives from each Governance Board, is responsible for writing and updating this manual. This role reviews and updates it as needed, at least once every 12 months.

For further information, please contact your Senior Director.

Quality Vision and Policy The Company Software Development is a leading provider of telecommunications software. We create business solutions that provide business value. If your project has an assigned ID resource, use the ID process and template for developing the Information Development Plan.

Our Mission 

  • To deliver business value. 
We will deliver business value as follows: 
  • We accomplish it within an Architectural Framework, 
  • We deliver consistent, timely and quality products, 
  • We operate in an environment that encourages teamwork, 
  • We embrace change and accept it as a normal part of our job, 
  • We understand that the keys to our success are our people, our processes and our partners.

Management Responsibility We have established a quality system based on quality management principles documented in this manual, the QMO, and in other organization-specific documents. This quality system is maintained by the Vice President of Quality role, which is currently being filled by representatives from each Governance Board, and implemented by the Network, Service Delivery and User Access COEs. 

Management responsibilities related to the Quality Management System are as follows: 

Chief Information Officer

The Chief Information Officer (CIO) of the company provides leadership concerning: 
  • Specific quality issues discussed at Senior Management Meetings 

General Manager - Software Development

The General Manager (GM) of Software Development: 
  • Provides guidance to COEs on quality goals 
  • Provides sponsorship for Transformation activities 

Network and Service Delivery/User Access Senior Directors

The Senior Directors: 
  • Own the Quality System 
  • Communicate the Quality Policy through this manual and at employee meetings 
  • Participate on the appropriate Governance Board 
  • Chair the strategic arm of the Quality Management Team 
  • Lead an annual review of the effectiveness and suitability of the Quality System 

Vice President - Quality Role

The Vice President of Quality role, has defined authority for: 
  • Ensuring that a quality system is established, implemented, and maintained 
  • Reporting on the performance of the Quality System to senior management for review and as a basis for improvement 
  • Sponsoring the Executive Quality Committee Meetings 
  • Owning this Quality Manual 
As part of the role of Vice President, of Quality the following responsibilities exist: 
  • Monitors the Quality System to make sure that it satisfies the requirements of the quality policy and each COE's quality objectives as stated in its quality system documentation 
  • Analyzes the effectiveness of the Quality System based on 
    • Client Feedback
    • Metrics
    • Financial reports 
    • Internal quality system audits 
    • Other sources 
  • Addresses quality regularly at the Governance Board meetings, and reports on the status of quality initiatives, quality problems, and the effectiveness of the quality system 
  • Generates action items for improving the quality system, which are documented as part of the meeting minutes of the Governance Board meetings and are reviewed at subsequent meetings.

Employees' Responsibility All regular, temporary, and contract employees, regardless of level, are responsible for Quality in their own areas. Subcontracted employees work under the supervision of management and/or lead technical or professional personnel. This means that all employees are responsible for the implementation of the QMS into their daily tasks and responsibilities as applicable. 

Each organization has identified a Quality representative for its system. This representative is responsible for developing processes and procedures to be applied on an organizational or project level. 

Each organization has defined a management review process to adequately maintain the effectiveness of the Quality System within their organization or projects.

Quality Approach The Network, Service Delivery and User Access COEs take a systems approach to Quality, based in part on the Software Engineering Institutes' Capability Maturity Model (CMM). This criteria provides a framework for continually improving our value to clients and improving our overall operational performance. 

The Quality System is documented in three tiers of standards, procedures, and guidelines: 
 
Tier 1  The Quality Manual (this document), provides an overview of the Network, Service Delivery and User Access COEs' Quality System. 
Tier 2  Quality Method of Operation (QMO) documents the software development methodology. 
Tier 3 Contains project-specific quality guidelines (local procedures).
