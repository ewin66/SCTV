Quality Management Team (QMT)

Overview The efficiency and appropriateness of the Quality Management System is determined by the Quality Management Team (QMT). This team is divided into two groups, each with a different focus, one strategic and one tactical. 
Governance Team The Network and Service Delivery/User Access (SD/UA) Governance Teams have the strategic responsibilities and consist of: 
  • Senior Directors from the Network COE 
  • Senior Directors from the SD/UA COE 
The Governance Teams are chartered to establish yearly quality goals and initiatives, annually review the effectiveness and suitability of the Quality Management System and vote on and approve QMO exceptions necessary to support new goals and initiatives. 
PIT  The tactical responsibilities of the QMT are comprised of the Network PIT and the SD/UA PIT. 

PITs are chartered to vote on and approve QMO change requests. 

The PITs ensure that change requests are implemented, and that information from lessons learned and best-in-class examples are disseminated. The members of the PITs partner on at least one internal audit annually. 
Responsibilities The QMT is responsible for: 
  • endorsing, defending and promoting the standard software development process as embodied in the QMO 
  • being accountable for the process results for their area(s) of responsibility 
  • addressing any and all implementation problems associated with the process 
  • ensuring the process stays up-to-date with technologies, and supports tool advances. 
Procedural Agreements Governance Teams: 
  • Formal Management Review of the effectiveness and suitability of the Quality System will occur once per year. Standing agenda for the yearly review is: 
    • Review current quality goals and initiatives 
    • Establish quality goals and initiatives for the coming year 
    • Review the suitability and effectiveness of the quality system based on the following: 
      • Customer satisfaction survey results 
      • Quarterly area metrics 
      • Internal Quality System Review findings 
      • External audit findings 
      • Customer measures 
      • Current progress of active quality initiatives 
      • "Grass roots" feedback on areas that could benefit from improvement 
      • Jeopardy/root cause analysis 
      • Risk Management common issues 
      • Input from the PIT 
  • Supporting meetings will concentrate on topics of interest related to the suitability and effectiveness of the quality system and other quality initiatives. 
  • Receive, approve or deny and maintain a record of QMO exceptions. 
  • Quality Records: Meeting Minutes and Updated Action Item Register. 
PIT : 
  • Provide input to the formal Management Review session. 
  • Approve QMO Change requests on a consensus basis. 
    • QMO Change Requests are tracked via a Change Control Log, maintained by the PITs. 
    • QMO Change Controls will be distributed to both PITs. Proactive approval or rejection is requested, however, no response within a designated timeframe will assume approval. 
    • If a consensus cannot be reached among both PITs, the request is not approved. 
    • Once consensus is reached among both PITs, the request is sent to the Governance Teams for their approval. 
  • General Meetings will have the following agenda: 
    • Review summary data from the Metrics program and Quality System Reviews 
    • Review the status of the quality goals 
    • Share Lessons Learned / Best Practices 
    • Review the status of action items 
    • Open Discussion 
  • Quality Records: Meeting Minutes and Updated Action Item Register, maintained by the PITs.
