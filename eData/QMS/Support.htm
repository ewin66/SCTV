7.1 Identify, Analyze, and Resolve Change Requests and Problems
Purpose  Clients who identify a problem with a project or have a request for technical assistance should report it to the appropriate organization so that the request can be analyzed and resolved. The procedures are defined in Corporate Problem Management Process (CPM).
Entrance Criteria  Technical request reported 
Steps 
  1. For all reported client problems, follow the procedures stated in the Corporate Problem Management process. 
  2. Request and distribute problem activity reports monthly (minimum), e.g., from PCRM, DDTS. 
Exit Criteria  Request status updated or closed
CR entered (if applicable) 
Quality Records  Activity Reports
Client CR Records 
Controlled Documents  None 
Metrics  CRs entered, by severity, including a Root Cause 
Templates  None 
Tools Change Control tools: 
  • PCRM 
  • DDTS 
Techniques As Appropriate

Purpose 

Change Requests
(CRs) are analyzed to identify their impact and benefit, and then resolved
and deployed.

 










Entrance
Criteria 

CRs 









Steps 



    

  1. 
Review and schedule CRs. 
    2. 


    A CR under review may be put on hold,
scheduled for a later release, or scheduled immediately. 

    For proper review, the participants
need to ensure the CR is clearly specified. 

    Perform CR analysis by: 

      

    • 
reviewing related documentation, e.g.,
CR descriptions, requirements, etc. to gain an understanding of change. 
      • 


    • 
identifying areas to be changed, e.g.,
functions, data, procedures, documentation, databases, security, batch/recovery,
test cases, etc. to determine the full impact of the change. 
      • 

    


  2. 
Schedule CR for a project release based
on several criteria: 
    3. 


      

    • 
high severity - need a fix within a short
time-frame (CPM Process resolution time guidelines) 
      • 


    • 
logical groupings of CRs 
      • 


    • 
availability of resources 
      • 


    • 
limiting the frequency of maintenance
releases 
      • 


    • 
etc. 
      •

are included in the corresponding Project Plan and tracked with that project.
