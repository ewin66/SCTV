Baseline Process

Purpose  Baselining is identifying a "base" from which subsequent work can proceed. Baselining ensures that there is agreement on the contents of a document or other artifact. After baselining, all changes to the "base" must undergo Change Control.

 Baselining is done after the action items from the reviews are resolved.

 
Entrance Criteria Draft Document or other artifact 
Steps 
  1. Identify people who need to baseline the document. Typically these people are representatives from the Project Team, key stakeholders, and/or any person impacted by the baseline decision (Author). 
  2. Schedule the baseline review and disseminate the material in sufficient time to prepare for review (Author). 
  3. Conduct a review to baseline the document, noting concerns and action items. Use the Review Tracking Sheet (Author). Writing Meeting Minutes might not always be necessary, in which case the Review Tracking Sheet and completed Action Item Register are sufficient for your quality records. Code Inspections suffices for software. 
  4. Accept the reviewed document and consider it baselined (Meeting Participants

     OR

     Review again after the open items or action items are resolved (Meeting Participants).
     
     

  5. Retain the appropriate artifacts in the Project Library for easy retrieval (Project Manager).
The document or other artifact is now a controlled document under Change Control.

Change Management
Purpose  Change management refers to services provided to support delivery of U S WEST IT software. These services are provided during the development, testing and support phases of a development release cycle.

 
Entrance Criteria Change Request or Trouble Report requiring code check-in 
Steps
  1. Provide Change Management Coordination Services: 
    • Rebase release libraries, merge libraries, archive releases, allocate releases (including setting up test releases). 
    • Resolve module status problems. 
    • Verify space, e.g., disk space. 
    • Audit release builds (comparison). 
    • Establish emergency release. 
    • Handle action item from development (including module deletion) to keep configuration management data bases current with development cycles. 
    • Handle client-specific and out-of-cycle support. 
  2. Provide build support: 
    • Schedule and coordinate module compilation with a project's development and test organization. 
    • Verify successful completion of build work requests and resolve issues. 
  3. Perform release packaging and software shipment. 
  4. Maintain the integrity reports to verify the correctness and completeness of the system shipped and verification that the system was shipped. 
Exit Criteria None 
Quality Records  Integrity Report 
Controlled Documents None 
Metrics None 
Templates None

Configuration Management 
Purpose  Configuration management provides a mechanism for identifying, controlling, and tracking the versions of each software item. It should: 
  • control sequential updating of a given software entity by more than one person 
  • uniquely identify the version of each software item 
  • identify the versions of each software item which together constitute a specific version of a complete project 
  • identify the build status of software entities in development, test, or delivered (deployed) and installed 
  • provide coordination for the updating of multiple software items in one or more locations as required 
  • identify and track all actions and changes resulting from a change request, from initiation through to release. 
Entrance Criteria None 
Steps 
  1. Develop a Configuration Management Plan that includes: 
    • organizations involved and their responsibilities 
    • configuration management activities 
    • configuration management tools, techniques and methodologies 
    • the stage at which items need to be brought under configuration control. 
  2. Review and baseline the Configuration Management Plan. See Baseline Process, Change Control Process, Document Control, and Reviews in the Appendix for details.

Quality Improvement Plan (QUIP)
Purpose A Quality Improvement Plan (QUIP) documents the areas where quality improvement is needed and the action items or activities necessary to improve a software project. The Project Manager is responsible for the QUIP.

 
Entrance Criteria  None 
Steps 
  1. Use appropriate sources of information such as the process, quality goals, audit results, findings from Lessons Learned Sessions, Client Feedback results, Root Cause Analysis data, or any other sources of corrective or preventive measures to create or update the QUIP (Project Manager). 
  2. Share the contents and goals of the QUIP with team members (Project Manager).
  3. Update the QUIP to include the action plans and associated objectives (Project Manager).
  4. Maintain an Action Item Register that includes clearly worded action items, defined accountability, major milestones, and measures of success. Action items should clearly indicate what steps will be taken to ensure that improvement is applied to future releases. Action items should be updated regularly (at minimum, quarterly) and shared with the team. Updates should include progress on measures of success (Project Manager).
  5. Discuss status at Project Status Meetings to monitor the progress on current goals and identify continuous quality improvement opportunities (Project Manager).
Exit Criteria None

Reviews
Purpose  Reviews give team members the opportunity to critique a document or artifact, solicit feedback, raise issues, and ensure integration with affected areas and stakeholders. Reviews may be one of two types: 

Informal reviews have one person serving multiple roles, and may not involve a meeting. Concurrence by stakeholders may be obtained via email. Stakeholders need to be notified when receiving the document or artifact where no response by a specified date will assume concurrence. At a minimum, 80% of the stakeholders must sign a Review Tracking Sheet indicating they have reviewed the document. 

Formal reviews may require appointing a moderator, reader, recorder, and reviewers before the meeting. Examples of formal review practices include Fagan or Clean Room reviews. 
Entrance Criteria None 
Steps 
  1. Publish a copy of the document or artifact for comments. 
  2. Conduct the review using the Review Tracking Sheet. 

    3. Subsequent reviews may be required until reviewers concur and all recorded action items have been closed. 
  3. Distribute approved document or artifact. 
Exit Criteria  Approved document or artifact 
Quality Records  Review Tracking Sheet
Meeting minutes (if applicable) 
Completed Action Item Register (if applicable) 
Controlled Documents Dependent on document being reviewed 
Metrics None 
Templates  Review Tracking Sheet

Root Cause Analysis
Purpose  Root Cause Analysis is performed to improve software development through the analysis of defects or other process indicators.

 The goal of root cause analysis is to recommend process changes that: 

  • prevent a problem from recurring, and 
  • aid in the detection of a problem earlier in the life cycle. 
Root cause analysis may be conducted in any phase of the life cycle.

 
Entrance Criteria None 
Steps
  1. Project management assigns qualified people to a Root Cause Analysis (RCA) Team or Defect Analysis Team (DAT). 
  2. The RCA team or DAT: 
    • identifies the kind of defects to be analyzed (field faults, system test errors, process indicators, etc.) 
    • establishes the frequency of the analysis. 
  3. Individual SMEs provide information which: 
    • describes the characteristics of the individual problem 
      • Where in the process did it occur? 
      • To what degree was the problem significant? 
    • suggests the root cause 
    • recommends prevention measure(s) 
    • recommends detection measure(s). 
  4. RCA team or DAT: 
    • groups the results of the analyses provided by "process experts" 
    • identifies common root causes 
    • documents the results 
    • provides recommendations for process changes or workable solutions to identified problems. 
  5. Project Management 
    • identifies action items to implement RCA team or DAT recommendation(s), or 
    • assigns appropriate recommendations for implementation to the project's Quality Improvement Plan (QUIP). 
Exit Criteria None 
Quality Records  Meeting minutes and completed Action Item Register 
Controlled Documents None 
Metrics None

Subcontractor/Supplier Management
Purpose  Subcontractor/Supplier management ensures that the deliverables to U S WEST meet the same quality standard as a U S WEST deliverable and that qualified subcontractors are selected and managed effectively. This applies to both software subcontracts, as well as to the management of the software component of a subcontract that includes software, hardware, and integration. Reference the U S WEST Policy on Software Subcontract Management - TPSP 12-03-1-08. 
Steps
  1. Select a subcontractor based on ability to perform the work (Account Manager, Project Manager, Client). This is based on many factors, like strategic business alliances, technical considerations, preferred subcontractors, purchasing, etc. This includes going through the procurement process. 
  2. Appoint a subcontract manager for the subcontractor (Project Manager). 
  3. Document an agreement covering the technical, as well as the non-technical (milestones, intermediate results, metrics, acceptance criteria, etc.) requirements and use this as a basis for managing the subcontract. Ensure that there are standards to be met, and consequences if those standards are not met. Use Analyst or Development to help define (or review) the technical scope to be subcontracted. Use Legal, Purchasing/Procurement, Finance to establish the requirements of the subcontract. The following lists identify required and suggested requirements to place upon a subcontractor: (Project Manager) 

    4.  

     
     
     

    Required: 

    • must have a project plan 
    • must have quality assurance activities that will be reviewed periodically by U S WEST 
    • must have configuration management activities that will be reviewed periodically by U S WEST 
    Suggested: 
    • must have a defined and documented design, development, test, and technical support process. At the request of U S WEST , subcontractor will provide a written statement of such documentaton. 
    • must perform formal software release, upgrade, and patch testing before shipment of software to clients, and such test cases should be documented and retained for review by U S WEST for up to one year from date of last test. 
    • must have a problem report tracking and resolution process or procedure that maintains history and can provide status of reported problems at all times, including problem description and resolution information. 
    • must have design documentation that demonstrates how a software product will be developed. Design documentation must be created before the product is implemented. Design documentation should be updated to reflect any significant design impacting changes that occur during implementation. 
    • Must be able to support claims that product meets specification, inclusive of standards and requirements, by demonstrating conformance. 
  4. Review the subcontract with all of the affected organizations (Subcontractor Manager). 
  5. Perform traditional monitoring and controlling activities, see Project Status Meetings to monitor the progress of the subcontract with both internal and external focus (Subcontractor Manager). 

    6. Audits by 3rd party auditors, and other techniques are encouraged.
    Ensure proper attention is given to interfaces and hand-offs. 
  6. Report status of the subcontract to team members at regularly scheduled Project Status Meetings(Subcontractor Manager). 
  7. Verify deliverables of the subcontract meet the predefined acceptance criteria. 
Entrance Criteria None

Training
Purpose  Training ensures that the personnel performing specific, assigned tasks are qualified on the basis of appropriate education, training, or experience.

 Anyone who holds a title or fills a role mentioned in the QMO is expected to have the skills required for that title or role. These skills may be acquired through formal training and/or on-the-job training.

 It is the responsibility of the person and the supervisor to identify required skills for a position and work with the supervisor to obtain training. These skills should be kept in a separate document, in additon to the performance review documentation. 
Entrance Criteria None 
Steps 
  1. Supervisors develop an individual training plan with their employees to identify weak areas or new skills necessary, dictated by: 
    • business strategy 
    • project strategy 
    • gaps between position needs and skills 
    • U S WEST IT directive. 
    Both the supervisor and employee have the responsibility to develop training plans. 

    The performance review documentation may not be used as the individual's training plan. 

  2. Register for the training. 
  3. Obtain training. 
  4. Retain the certificate or other confirmation of the acquired skill. Update the training plan accordingly. 
Exit Criteria None 
Quality Records  Individual's Training Plan
Controlled Documents None 
Metrics None 
Templates Training Plan
